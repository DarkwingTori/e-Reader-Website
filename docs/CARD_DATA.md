# CARD_DATA.md

## e-Reader Card Series Reference

A reference guide for populating the card database. Use this alongside the sources listed at the bottom of this file.
The extensive guide for English is usa_card_list

---

## Hardware Versions

| Version | Region | Release Date | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Card-e Reader (original) | JP only | Dec 1, 2001 | No link cable port, no internal memory |
| e-Reader (NA redesign) | US, AU | Sep 2002 | Added link cable passthrough, internal memory |
| Card-e Reader+ | JP | Jun 27, 2003 | JP release of the updated version |

> Japanese e-Readers cannot read foreign cards. Foreign e-Readers cannot read Japanese cards.

## Card Series by Game

### Animal Crossing-e (US)
- **Total cards:** 328
- **Release:** October 2002 – May 2003 (4 series of booster packs)
- **Price:** $2.99 per booster pack
- **Card types:** Character Cards, Design Cards, Town Tune Cards, Classic Game Cards (Ice Climber, Mario Bros.)
- **Promo cards:** 1 bundled with e-Reader, 1 in Nintendo Power #163

### Doubutsu no Mori+ Card-e (JP)
- Predecessor to Animal Crossing-e
- Most cards localized into Animal Crossing-e (all except card #252 Katrina)

### Doubutsu no Mori Card-e+ (JP)
- **Total cards:** 108
- **Release:** June – October 2003 (3 series)
- **Card types:** 84 Character Cards, 12 Town Tune Cards, 12 Design Cards
- **New to this set:** D09 Falcon Suit, D11 DK Logo design cards

### Super Mario Advance 4: Super Mario Bros. 3-e (US/JP)
- **Card types:** Power-Up Cards, Demo Cards, Level Cards
- **Bundled:** 2 cards included with every US/AU copy
- **Walmart exclusive:** 5 additional cards — extremely rare, e-Reader discontinued shortly after
- **JP exclusive levels:** Many level cards never released outside Japan
  - Later unlocked in Wii U Virtual Console and Nintendo Switch Online versions

### Pokémon Battle-e (US)
- Compatible with Pokémon Ruby and Sapphire
- Allows scanning trainer battles, receiving berries, decorations, and the Berry glitch fix
- TCG sets with dot codes: Expedition Base Set, Aquapolis, Skyridge

### Pokémon-e TCG: EX Ruby & Sapphire (US)
- **Release:** June 18, 2003
- **Total cards:** 210 unique, 109 cards, 65 with dot codes
- **Booster pack price:** $3.29 (9 cards), $9.99 (61-card pre-constructed deck)
- Cards #1–14 are special foil cards without dot codes

### EON Ticket (US Promo)
- Distributed at: E3 2003, Toys R Us EON Ticket Summer Tour, Nintendo Power #173
- Unlocks Southern Island in Pokémon Ruby / Sapphire
- Ruby: receive Latias. Sapphire: receive Latios
- E3 variant has gold E3 stamp on front

### NES Classic Game Cards (US)
- NES games encoded across 9–10 dot code cards (2 per card)
- Bundled with e-Reader: Donkey Kong Jr. or Pinball (depending on pack)
- Notable titles: Balloon Fight, Excitebike, Donkey Kong Jr., Pinball, Manhole (Game & Watch)

### Mario Party-e (US)
- Portable board game — e-Reader not required for main game
- Some cards contain scannable minigames

### Mario vs. Donkey Kong (JP only)
- CoroCoro Comic competition — only 1,000 people won card sets
- 6 cards released (data for 12 levels exists in all versions, accessible via Action Replay)
- Among the rarest e-cards in existence

### Pikmin 2-e (JP only)
- 6 packs of minigames released alongside Pikmin 2
- Region-specific encoding — cannot be used with non-Japanese e-Readers

### Mega Man Battle Network (JP only — e-Reader+)
- Rockman EXE 4, 5, 6 mod cards
- Effects: Base HP, Abilities, Buster Changes, Charge Shot Modifications
- Item cards: Battle Chips, Sub Chips, BugFrags, Zenny, Navi Customizer Programs
- EXE6 mod cards removed from EU/US versions entirely
- Content now accessible in Mega Man Battle Network Legacy Collection

### Mega Man Zero 3 (JP only — e-Reader+)
- Content now accessible in Mega Man Zero/ZX Legacy Collection

### Pokémon Channel Promos (US/AU)
- US: "The Pikachu Star", "The Kyogre Constellation", "Poké A la Card"
- AU variant: Jirachi replaces Kyogre Constellation

### Hamtaro (JP)
- Series of minigame cards requiring specific character card combinations
- Fully Japan-exclusive

## Rarity Tiers

### Ultra Rare
- Mario vs. Donkey Kong cards (1,000 JP contest winners only)
- E3 2002 Promo Pack (Manhole variant, Pokémon TCG cards, Kirby prize card)
- E3 2003 EON Ticket (gold stamp variant)
- Walmart-exclusive SMA4 cards (5 cards, discontinued shortly after release)

### Rare
- EON Ticket (Toys R Us / Nintendo Power variants)
- Pokémon Channel promo cards
- Australian e-Reader launch cards (Air Hockey variant, Manhole variant)

### Uncommon
- Nintendo Power exclusive cards
- FoxBox Kirby Slide Puzzle (Nintendo Power #175, Tips & Tricks)
- NES Classic game cards (complete sets)

### Common
- Standard Animal Crossing-e booster packs (Series 1–4)
- Pokémon-e TCG booster packs
- Mario Party-e cards

## Canceled / Unreleased
- Complete Game & Watch card series (planned, never released)
  - Would have included remakes similar to Game & Watch Gallery series
- European e-Reader (announced, canceled July 2004)
- Mario Kart: Super Circuit e-Reader compatibility (suggested on official website, never confirmed)
- Remaining 6 Mario vs. DK levels (data exists in all versions, cards never produced outside JP contest)
